This folder contains some references concerning the principle of maximum entropy which I personally find insightful. (It is not yet a complete list.)

The most significant:


The original: [Jaynes 1957](https://darsakthi.github.io/files/jaynes/Jaynes1957.pdf). 

The first optimality proof: [Shore--Johnson 1980](https://darsakthi.github.io/files/jaynes/SJ80.pdf).

More about coarse graining: [Jaynes 1985](https://darsakthi.github.io/files/jaynes/Jaynes1985.pdf).





More resources:

A comprehensive review of the state of the art then: [Jaynes 1978](https://darsakthi.github.io/files/jaynes/Jaynes1978.pdf)

> In May 1978 a three-day Symposium on 'The Maximum-Entropy Formalism' held at M.I.T. provided a good opportunity to put into the record a more comprehensive summing up than had been attempted until then.

The relations to Bayesian inference: [Jaynes1982](https://darsakthi.github.io/files/jaynes/Jaynes1982.pdf) and [Jaynes 1988](https://darsakthi.github.io/files/jaynes/Jaynes1988.pdf)

A critical discussion of its limitations when applied to inferring properties of non-equilibrium systems: [GGR2019](https://darsakthi.github.io/files/jaynes/GGR2019.pdf)
